Let's Talk About Painting

Welcome to Painting For Beginners. A blog dedicated to teaching you all about painting. First things first, painting is lots of fun. There's something so freeing about using a paint brush to create something. Using my tips and tricks, you can learn how to paint hassle-free!


Painting is an artistic skill that anyone at any time of their life. Personally, I started painting in my tween years and I've been in love with it ever since. It does take some skills in drawing to excell in painting. So, with some basic skills in drawing, it's time to pick up a brush, get your paints and start!

Tools

Of course, to paint you'll need tools so here are some things you'll need to start painting:

✦ Sketchbooks

    Having a sketchbook handy is great for sketching painting ideas. You can sketch with pencils, pens, markers, etc.

✦ Paint Brushes/Knives


    I recommend having a variety of sized paintbrushes and some large paint brushes while painting. Round brushes, flat brushes, filbert brushes, rigger brushes, fan brushes, to name a few. Paint knives are also a great tool for scraping paint off your palette, mixing your paints, or adding texture to your painting.
Brush Name Description
Round Brush Great for details and filling in color.
Flat Brush Perfect for underpainting/paint washes. Smooth application.
Filbert Brush A mix of flat and round brush, great for round edges and shapes.
Rigger/Liner Brush Fine, long tipped brush for flowing lines and lettering.
Angled Brush Versatile for thick or thin lines and details.
Fan Brush Wide brush with fanned bristles, great for quick details and texture.

✦ Paint Palettes

    It is essential to have a paint palette while painting. It's the place where you'll store your paints and mix paints. Either wooden, plastic, glass or wax paper, you definetely need a paint palette handy while working.
