Types of Paints

Time to talk about the different kinds of paints. Spoilers, there's more than you think!

    ✦ Watercolor paint

One of the most common types of paint is watercolor paint. You might have used water-activated paints in school growing up, as have I! Water-activated watercolors come in seperate pans already dry. The paints become Water-activated once water is added to it, either using a wet brush or sprayed with water. There is also tubed watercolors, where the paints are wet in its own paint tube. And lastly watercolor inks, which are stored in glass bottles with a dropper.

    ✦ Tempera paint

Tempera paint was my first step into painting besides water-activated watercolors I used in school. Tempera paint is a fast-drying paint, water soluble paint made of egg yolks. Yes, egg yolks. One of the materials that make tempera paint are egg yolks. Tempera paint is one of the earliest paints we've ever used in history, dating back to 1200 BCE!

    ✦ Acrylic paint

Acrylic paint is another common kind of paint we use. There are water and latex based acrylic paints. Like tempera, acrylic paint is a fast-drying paint. You can change the texture and opacity all depending on the amount of water is mixed with acrylic paint. How cool!

    ✦ Gouache paint

Gouache paint is best described as a mix of watercolor and acrylic paint as it can behave like either paints! With gouache, if you add a little bit of added water you have a watercolor consistency and appearance. A bit less added water, and you got acrylic paint consistency.

    ✦ Oil paint

Oil paint dries a lot slower than the above paints. It can take days for a painting to completely dry! Oil paint typically requires additional liquids and oils to achieve that slow-dry and that glossy look. It is vital to use oil paint in a well ventilated area and have proper PPE while using it.

    ✦ Spray paint

Spray paint or aerosol paint comes in a pressurized can and is released as a spray. Commonly used in street art, such as murals or for stencils and furniture. To use spray paint, you should wear masks and gloves.

    ✦ Encaustic paint

You might not have heard of encaustic paint before, but chances are you might have seen artwork that used it in museums! Encaustic paint is a form of painting that involved hot wax and heat. Developed by the ancient Greeks, encaustic paint is a technical form of painting that requires heat. to manipulate the paint.
